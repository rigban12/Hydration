💧 The Science of Hydration

Evidence-based facts about water and your body, sourced from globally recognised health organisations.

🧠

Brain & Cognitive Function

Your brain is approximately 75% water. Studies show that losing just 1–2% of your body's water content can impair concentration, short-term memory, and decision-making speed by up to 30%.

WHO · Harvard Health 2023
💪

Physical Performance

Even mild dehydration of 2% body weight loss can reduce physical endurance by up to 20%, impair strength, and increase perceived effort during exercise or active work.

NHS · Journal of Strength & Conditioning
❤️

Heart Health

Adequate hydration helps the heart pump blood more efficiently. People who drink enough water daily have a significantly lower risk of developing heart failure, according to NIH research.

NIH · European Heart Journal 2022
🫁

Kidney Protection

Drinking sufficient water is the most effective way to prevent kidney stones. Staying well-hydrated can reduce risk by up to 50% by diluting minerals that form stones.

NHS · Mayo Clinic
🌡️

Temperature Regulation

Water is critical to thermoregulation. The body uses sweat — primarily water — to cool itself. Dehydration impairs this mechanism, increasing risk of heat stress in warm offices and travel.

WHO · CDC Heat Safety 2023
😴

Sleep & Recovery

Dehydration is linked to shorter sleep duration and poorer sleep quality. Maintaining hydration throughout the day supports melatonin production and overnight cellular repair.

Sleep Foundation · NIH 2023
🩺

Daily Intake Recommendations

The European Food Safety Authority recommends 2.0L/day for women and 2.5L/day for men from all beverages and food. The NHS recommends 6–8 glasses (roughly 1.5–2L) of fluid per day.

EFSA · NHS Eatwell Guide
🔬

Metabolism & Energy

Water is essential in nearly every metabolic reaction. Drinking 500ml of water can boost metabolic rate by up to 30% for 30–40 minutes, according to a study in the Journal of Clinical Endocrinology.

J. Clinical Endocrinology · 2022
🌿

Skin & Immune Health

Proper hydration maintains skin elasticity, supports lymphatic function, and helps flush toxins. The skin is the body's largest organ and requires consistent water intake to function as a barrier.

British Skin Foundation · NHS
